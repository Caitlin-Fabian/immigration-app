Interview Time!

  1. You meet with a U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services worker

  2. You take the English and civics tests

    If you need medical exception to the English and civics testing requirements, submit Form N-648, Medical Certification for Disability Exceptions if you did not submit it earlier.
    For more info on exceptions and accommodations visit this link.

    This link goes to the dedicated study section for the English and civics tests.

  3. USCIS officer might decide to continue your case

    This does not mean you are denied. The USCIS officer will continue your case and may ask for more evidence, additional documents, or a second interview.
    This also might happen if you fail the English or civics test, or both. If this happens, the officer will schedule you to come back for another interview in the next 60-90 days. They will only test you on the part that you failed. Unfortunely, you are only allowed 2 chances. So, make sure to study for the test!

  4. USCIS will notify you of the interview results following your interview

    This means that the officer will continue your case and may ask for more evidence or a second interview.

  • Granted

    USCIS approves your Form N-400 if you are eligible for naturalization through the evidence on record.

  • Denied

    USCIS denied your Form N-400 because you are not eligible for naturalization based on the evidence provided.

    This does not mean its over. If you believe that the USCIS incorrectly denied your Form N-400, you can take it to court in hopes to appeal the decision. The instructions on how will be on the notice you received. You can also visit this link for more information.
    You MUST file Form N-336 with the appropriate fee within 30 days from your decision date. If not, the decision is final!

  1. Bring all the documents with you

    Collect the required documentation and anything that will help your case. You should also make copies of all your documentation. Be organized!
    - State ID, drivers licence
    -Passports - USCIS-issued travel documents
    - Marriage certificate
    - Divorse papers
    - Death certificates

  2. Look Presentable

    There is no dress code, but you should choose business casual attire that represents you well. First impressions are very important.
    For a more indepth article on what business casual actually is go here.
    Be on Time! You should arrive 15 minutes early.

  3. Dont be afriad to ask the Officer to repeat the question if you do not understand

    It is important to understand the question properly and answer promptly. Ask them to repeat the question if you could not hear the officer or can't understand.

  4. Be Yourself!

    The USCIS officer wants to know your story. It is important to represent yourself in a professional and genuine way. You got this!
